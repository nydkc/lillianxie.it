Introduction

Hey Key Clubbers!

My name is Lillian Xie, and I’m currently a junior at Hunter College High School. This year, I’m running to be one of your 2014-2015 Key Club International Trustees. I’ve been part of the K-Family for five years now, and this year is my third as a proud member of Key Club International. As trustee, I aim to improve communication, education, representation, and unification to create a stronger community based on the common values of service and leadership development.

This past year, I’ve learned how to communicate with individuals on different levels of the organization, ranging from International officers to club members in my local area. As the Immediate Past Distinguished Lieutenant Governor of Division 11 of NYDKC, I loved being a servant leader and helping the members and officers within my division grow as Key Clubbers and as individuals. In running for International Office, I want to encourage others to share the same passion for service, and I want to reach out to individuals across districts to strengthen our organization as a whole.

So, trust in me to be your trustee! #LillianforIT

FAQ

How do you pronounce your last name?

Why did you join Key Club?

A few years ago, I attended my first Club Open House event in my school’s auditorium as an incoming student, where the Key Club panel inspired me to attend the first club meeting. Within the first few minutes, I was hooked. Not only did the existing club members show how dedicated they were to service, but also I noticed how close of a family the club was. Everyone knew one another and held a common value of helping the community. As a younger student who had no idea which direction to take in life, I knew that Key Club would help me along the way. And since then, I've tried my best to give back to the organization that has given me so much.

In what ways do you think your Key Club experience has helped you as a person academically, spiritually, socially, etc?

Before I joined Key Club, I was extremely shy and could barely speak to individual people, let alone large crowds. I tended to keep to myself and avoid social situations because I really didn’t know how to act in front of people. However, after a few Key Club meetings in my home club (go Hunter!), I started to open up to the inspirational seniors in my school. They encouraged me to talk, motivated me to participate, and later, inspired me to lead. Since then, because holding an office in Key Club does take a substantial amount of time, my experience has taught me better time management skills. Now, I organize my week-to-week, day-to-day, hour-to-hour schedule with a small, color-coded notebook that I keep on me at all times.

What do you hope to accomplish with Key Club International?

My goals as International Trustee would be to improve communication, promote education, embody representation, and foster unification. My own personal goal is to serve as an efficient and caring servant leader to help members serve their communities to the best of their abilities and to spread our motto to new potential members: "Caring—Our Way of Life."

Why aren’t you in the Candidates Booklet?

My faculty advisor took an unexpected, long absence, and I was told that she would return by the deadline for paperwork submission, but immediately prior to her expected date of return, she extended her leave. Because of these circumstances, I was unable to submit my paperwork by the deadline, and was late by one day.
